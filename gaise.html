--- title: "Little Apps and GAISE" --- ## DRAFT The 2016 [GAISE report](https://www.amstat.org/asa/files/pdfs/GAISE/GaiseCollege_Full.pdf) provides guidelines for teaching introductory statistics that many instructors have found useful in updating their own courses. 1. Teach statistical thinking. a. Teach statistics as an investigative process problem-solving and decision-making. b. Give students experience with multivariable thinking. 2. Focus on conceptual understanding. 3. Integrate real data with a context and purpose. 4. Foster active learning. 5. Use technology to explore concepts and analyze data. 6. Use assessments to improve and evaluate student learning. Every Little App involves selecting a data set and producing a random sample of selected variables from that data set. The sample will be graphed and summarized in various ways. The Little Apps make it easy to draw a new sample, allowing the user to quickly get an impression of the extent of sampling variability.
